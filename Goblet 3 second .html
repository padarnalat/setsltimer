Man and the Secrets of Nearness

A Simple Guide for Students

This book teaches that humans are created to understand God, but inside us there is a struggle between Soul, Heart, Ego, and Satan.

Theme 1: Understanding Humans and God's Greatness

Chapter 1: Finding Glorious God's Greatness in Things
  • Humans are intelligent, but our knowledge is very small compared to God's knowledge.
  • When we look at the universe, we realize how powerful and perfect God is.
  • This chapter teaches humility — we should not think we know everything.
Chapter 2: The Search for Man and his Real Substance
  • Humans easily become attached to things like money, people, and comfort.
  • But they forget the most important connection — their relationship with God.
  • This chapter reminds us: our true purpose is not the world, but knowing God.
Chapter 3: The Secret of Union
  • Getting close to God is possible for everyone.
  • It requires thinking, observing, and doing good actions.
  • It is not instant — it needs effort and sincerity.
Chapter 4: Arguments for Proving the Existence of Almighty God through Logic
  • Nothing can exist without a creator.
  • If small things like phones need makers, the universe must also have a Creator.
  • Denying God is not logical when we see design everywhere.
Chapter 5: The Observation of Glorious God's Greatness in Things
  • Nature is full of amazing examples of God's power.
  • Even a tiny seed can grow into a large tree.
  • This shows that God controls everything, even things we cannot see.
Chapter 6: The Privileges of Man's Intellectual Position and the Secrets of his Success
  • Humans are given intelligence, memory, and creativity.
  • But many people misuse these abilities for harm instead of good.
  • True success is using knowledge for good and remembering God.
Chapter 7: Man's Trajectory of Journey throughout Knowledge and its Reality
  • Life is like a journey with a beginning and an end.
  • We are here for a short time to make choices.
  • Those choices decide our future after death.

Theme 2: The Soul

Chapter 8: Soul, We, and the Key to Other World's Truths
  • The soul helps us understand truth beyond what we see.
  • Along with the heart and mind, it guides our decisions.
  • If we ignore it, we live only for physical things.
Chapter 9: Some of the Signs of the Heavens in Mankind
  • Humans have desires that reflect something greater.
  • We love beauty, speed, and happiness.
  • This shows we are made for something beyond this world.
Chapter 10: What did We Do that We did not Experience?
  • Humans invent amazing things like machines and technology.
  • But we often forget to understand ourselves.
  • This chapter teaches self-awareness.
Chapter 11: The Characteristics and States of Soul in Man's Outer and Inner
  • The soul has different levels — good and bad.
  • Some people control their soul, others are controlled by it.
  • The goal is to reach a peaceful and balanced soul.

Theme 3: Heart

Chapter 12: What They See In the Computer of the Heart
  • The heart works like a control center.
  • If it is clean, it helps us make good choices.
  • If it is corrupted, it leads us to bad actions.
Chapter 13: Conditions of the Heart (Qalb) and Its Secrets
  • The heart can receive good or bad ideas.
  • It acts like a receiver picking signals.
  • We must protect it from negative influence.
Chapter 14: Not Acting upon the Secrets of the Heart, What did we do?
  • Humans achieve great things in the world.
  • But they ignore their inner problems.
  • This leads to imbalance and confusion.

Theme 4: Ego

Chapter 15: Us and Vigilance, Ego and Deceitfulness
  • Ego makes us selfish and proud.
  • It tricks us into thinking we are always right.
  • We must stay aware to control it.
Chapter 16: Ego and Its Plans
  • Ego works slowly and builds bad habits.
  • It pushes us toward comfort and laziness.
  • If uncontrolled, it leads to bad behavior.
Chapter 17: If these Things had Happened, What Things would not have Happened?
  • Humans can control machines and animals.
  • But they struggle to control themselves.
  • This shows the importance of self-discipline.

Theme 5: Satan

Chapter 18: Get to Know Satan and His Satanic Ways
  • Satan does not force people — he whispers ideas.
  • He makes wrong things look normal or good.
  • This is how people slowly go in the wrong direction.
Chapter 19: Man and Some of Satan's Plots
  • Satan distracts us with useless things.
  • He uses our emotions to control us.
  • The less aware we are, the easier we fall into his traps.

Final Summary: What Controls Human Life?

  • Soul: Pushes toward truth, goodness, and purpose.
  • Heart: Makes decisions — clean heart = good choices.
  • Ego: Pulls toward selfishness, pride, and comfort.
  • Satan: Uses ego to mislead slowly.

Final Result:

  • If Soul + Heart are strong → Peace, clarity, success
  • If Ego + Satan dominate → Confusion, bad decisions, regret
